About Me

Polymath
Builder
Curious

Hi! Here are the three facts you should know about me: I'm impressive, awesome, and hardworking. The fourth fact is that I have absolutely no problem telling people the first three facts. Some call it self-confidence, I call it advanced personal marketing. I'm also incredibly modest - I literally just proved that by telling you how awesome I am with a completely straight face.

Now, let's talk about what I do and what is this boring 'about me' section. Actually, I'm what people call a polymath or polyhistor - which sounds way fancier than it actually is. Fun fact: I didn't even know these terms existed until I asked ChatGPT 'What do people call a crazy person like me who's obsessed with learning everything?' Turns out there's an actual word for it, and it is not a mental illness.

From the beginning of time—or at least when I first arrived on this place called Terra, or Gaia, or Tellus, or Earth (I like to keep my options open)—I grew up wondering why most people only do one thing or specialize in one field. It's like they picked their favorite flavor of ice cream at age 5 and decided that was it for life. No vanilla-chocolate-strawberry-pistachio adventures allowed!

I started realizing this even more when I went to school, where everyone focused on memorizing stuff. As Mark Twain wisely said, "I have never let my schooling interfere with my education" though I had my own version: "Don't memorize the things that you can look up!" And yeah, that great man... or idiot.. was none other than me.(I'm still deciding which category I fall into.)

I asked many people about what they wanted to become or what they liked to do, and guess what? Most of the answers were doctor, engineer, pilot, and other conventional jobs. But I had a different answer—I wanted to do everything that I could. I was endlessly curious and never got bored. I wanted to build stuff, help people, solve problems, learn new things, but I never wanted to memorize thousands of pages like some kind of human photo copy machine.

Apparently, this made me weird. Or as I later discovered, a "polymath"—which sounds way cooler than "that person who can't pick a lane." Oh, you are still reading, I think I should write a biography about myself, what do you think. Ok, I think this much is fine. Now, you get a clear idea who I am.

Expert

Coding

Full-stack development is just a fancy way of saying "I can break things in multiple programming languages simultaneously." I build applications that solve real-world problems, though sometimes I create new problems in the process. It's like being a digital detective, except the crime scene is my own code.

Pro

Gaming

From competitive esports to indie gems, I explore interactive entertainment because someone needs to professionally procrastinate while calling it "research." I analyze game design like it's rocket science, which explains why I can strategize a perfect build in an RPG but still can't figure out why my laundry keeps multiplying.

MMA

Martial Arts

Discipline, technique, and philosophy through various martial arts practices. I study different traditions because apparently, I needed more ways to hurt myself while trying to achieve inner peace. It's like meditation, but with more bruises and philosophical discussions about whether that board really needed to be broken.

Analyst

Sports

Athletic excellence, strategy analysis, and the spirit of competition across various sports. I'm that person who can explain the perfect strategy while sitting on the couch eating chips. I analyze sports like a chess grandmaster, even though my own athletic abilities peak at enthusiastic spectating.

Scholar

Psychology

Understanding human behavior and cognitive processes is basically my attempt to figure out why people do weird things—including myself. I study mental wellness approaches because apparently, being a polymath comes with the side effect of overthinking everything.

Polyglot

Languages & Culture

Learning spoken languages and exploring diverse cultures because apparently, confusing myself in one language wasn't challenging enough. I wanted to expand my confusion globally. Each new language is like unlocking a secret level in the game of human communication.

Otaku

Japanese animation, light novels, and manga series because I needed a hobby that could make me cry over fictional characters while simultaneously teaching me life lessons. I study the rich storytelling culture of anime like it's literature class, except with more dramatic power-ups and fewer pop quizzes.

Creator

Video production, storytelling, and digital media creation across platforms. I create engaging content because apparently, having opinions about everything wasn't enough—I needed to film myself having those opinions too. It's like being a one-person entertainment industry, except with more technical difficulties.
